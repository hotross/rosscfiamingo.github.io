## the slightly longer version

Dash Labs is Ross Fiamingo, based in Brisbane. I've spent 15 years working across genuinely varied technical environments. CSIRO robotics, Rheinmetall defence systems, Hitachi mining hardware and biomedical research instrumentation at UQ. The through-line across all of it is that I'm the person who gets handed the thing that isn't working and is expected to figure it out.

I fix things rather than replace them. I automate things rather than do them twice. I genuinely enjoy debugging problems that other people have given up on. I've demonstrated autonomous robots to NASA representatives and federal ministers. I've deployed software to tank turret systems under defence security protocols. I've also spent a lot of time with a hot air rework station and a multimeter sorting out things nobody else wanted to look at. I like all of it equally.

Dash Labs exists because that kind of broad, deep technical experience is useful to a lot of businesses and individuals and it doesn't fit neatly into any one job title.

## credentials

Instrumentation and Control Trade Certificate from Townsville TAFE. Restricted Electrical License. Australian Government Baseline Security Clearance. 15 years of actual paid work in mining, defence, robotics and biomedical research. Not side projects. Real jobs with real stakes. PCB design in Altium and KiCAD. Advanced soldering including 0201 SMD rework. Linux, Python, Docker, Ansible, ROS. Used in production, not just listed on a CV.

You can see documented examples of past work at [hotross.github.io](https://hotross.github.io){:target="_blank"}. PCB builds, robotics deployments, home automation, server infrastructure and more.
